<img alt="" class="webfeedsFeaturedVisual wp-post-image" height="150" src="https://blog.zabbix.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/security_880x516-150x150.png" style="display: block; margin-bottom: 5px; clear: both;" width="150" /><p>Security has always been a core priority for us at Zabbix. As part of our ongoing commitment to delivering a reliable and secure monitoring platform, we regularly publish security <a href="https://www.zabbix.com/security_advisories">advisories</a> that reflect both newly discovered vulnerabilities and the improvements we&#8217;ve made to address them.</p>
<p><span id="more-32831"></span></p>
<p>These advisories are not just a list of issues &#8211; they are a direct result of continuous internal efforts to analyze, test, and strengthen every aspect of our product.</p>
<h4 id="more-than-just-fixes"><strong>More than just fixes</strong></h4>
<p>Every vulnerability we disclose represents a deeper process behind the scenes. It involves:</p>
<ul>
<li>Careful investigation and validation</li>
<li>Improvements to internal tooling and detection methods</li>
<li>Reevaluation of development and testing processes</li>
<li>Retesting to ensure robustness and prevent regressions</li>
</ul>
<p>For us, security is not a one-time fix &#8211; it&#8217;s an ongoing cycle of improvement.</p>
<h4 id="decoding-severity-scores"><strong>Decoding severity scores</strong></h4>
<p>We understand that some of the published severity scores may appear alarming at first glance. It&#8217;s important to note that these scores are based on worst-case scenario evaluations. In real-world deployments, the actual risk often depends on system configuration, network exposure, access controls, usage patterns, and more.</p>
<p>For many typical Zabbix installations, the effective risk level may be significantly lower than the maximum theoretical score suggests.</p>
<h4 id="stay-updated---it-matters"><strong>Stay updated &#8211; it matters</strong></h4>
<p>Keeping your Zabbix installation up to date is one of the most effective ways to maintain a secure environment.<br />
Each update includes not only bug fixes, but also: security enhancements, hardening improvements, and stability and performance updates. By applying updates regularly, you make sure that your systems benefit from the latest protections. In short, staying up to date is a shared responsibility and the best defense.</p>
<h4 id="open-communication"><strong>Open communication</strong></h4>
<p>We are aware that security advisories can sometimes lead to external reports that frame vulnerabilities without full context. We want to be clear that:</p>
<ul>
<li>Publishing advisories is a sign of maturity and transparency, not weakness</li>
<li>Proactively identifying and fixing issues is a core strength</li>
<li>Our goal is not to avoid disclosure, but to handle it responsibly and openly</li>
</ul>
<p>Security is not about the absence of vulnerabilities. It&#8217;s about how quickly and effectively they are identified, addressed, and communicated.</p>
<h4 id="going-forward"><strong>Going forward</strong></h4>
<p>We believe that transparency builds trust. If you have any questions about Zabbix security advisories or best practices, we encourage you to reach out to us. Our team is always ready to clarify, assist, and provide guidance. We remain fully committed to improving Zabbix security at every level &#8211; from code to processes to communication.</p>
<p>Your trust is important to us, and we will continue to invest in making Zabbix a secure and dependable platform for your infrastructure.</p>
